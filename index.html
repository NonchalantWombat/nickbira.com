Nicholas Bira, PhD

Body of Work

My name is Dr. Nicholas Bira, and I am a robotics researcher and engineer based in the Greater Boston Area. I hold a PhD in Robotics from Oregon State University, where I was an NSF Graduate Research Fellow focused on the design and control of compliant robotic systems using smart materials, including magnetorheological fluids and electropermanent magnets. Since then, I’ve built a career at the intersection of electromechanical design, soft robotics, materials science, and scalable manufacturing.

I currently work as a Senior Robotics and Magnetics Analytical Engineer at Fleet Robotics, where I lead the design, modeling, and fabrication of underwater robotic systems for ship hull inspection. My work integrates custom magnetics and electronics, finite element analysis, tribology, soft materials, robotic simulation enviornments, and production pipeline engineering. Previously, I served as a Postdoctoral Fellow at Harvard University’s BioDesign Lab, where I led product development for educational soft robotics systems and mentored student researchers. My experience spans all phases of R&D, from securing federal grants and mentoring teams to shipping robust, field-ready electromechanical systems. Across every project, I’m driven by a passion for solving hard problems at the edge of hardware and science.

Beyond professional robotics, I love making things. I’ve run a resin dice manufacturing business, collaborated on artisanal product design and manufacturing with Cantrip Candles in Los Angeles. I stay grounded through cooking, baking, and tabletop games like Dungeons and Dragons. Whether designing a robot or planning a fantasy adventure for my friends, I bring hands-on curiosity and a commitment to building things that matter.
